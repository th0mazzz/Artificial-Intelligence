Smart Same as Naive, but does preprocessing to determine valid guesses at the start of the board as to eliminate some incorrect paths

My report will be the following. I am surprised at the seemingly randomness of my smarter algorithm. It does preprocessing at the beginning so that it eliminates all invalid guesses from the get-go, thereby decreasing the amount of deadends that the solver needs to backtrack out of. It worked extremely well in some cases, but extremely poorly in other cases, which makes little sense to me. Because there are a ton of deadends in sudoku, I expected the smart program to have a larger impact on the results of all the sudoku boards; yet it only had drastic results on a limited few.
